## VOICE & TONE

### The Dlytful Voice Always:

**Sounds Like a Real Person** — Never corporate. Never passive. No "solutions" or "leveraging."

**Respects Intelligence** — We don't over-explain. Your audience understands nuance. We trust them.

**Feels Earned** — Every word does work. No fluff. No unnecessary qualifiers.

**Stays Grounded** — We let the work speak. We don't need to keep naming the thing we're doing.

### Tone Variations:

**On Copy:** Warm. Direct. Natural. Example: "Your product already has a soul. We help it speak."

**In Emails:** Like a text from someone you respect. No "Hi there!" or corporate pleasantries.

**In Pricing/CTAs:** Clear without trying to convince. "Start positioning your product" not "Unlock your potential."

**In Documentation:** Practical and human. Explanatory without condescension.

- **Negative space is deliberate.** Empty areas are choices, not mistakes.

## WRITING FRAMEWORK FOR YOUR CLIENTS

When you're consulting a developer, this is how you translate their product into brand language:

### 1. THE DISCOVERY CONVERSATION (15 minutes)

Ask them:
- "Walk me through the moment someone uses this and thinks 'oh, this is good.' What's happening?"
- "What feeling do you want someone to have when they first encounter your product?"
- "What would your actual early users say about it to their friends—in their own words?"
- "What are you deliberately *not* trying to be?"

**Listen for:** The emotional moment, not the feature benefit. The feeling of relief, clarity, ease, or delight.

### 2. EXTRACT THE SIGNAL (10 minutes)

Pull out:
- **One core feeling** (not a feature; a feeling)
- **The moment it solves** (not the problem; the exact moment when it matters)
- **The person it's for** (specific enough to imagine their desk)
- **What using it signals about them** (what does it say that they chose this?)

### 3. BUILD THE TRANSLATION

Create:
- **Positioning:** Short, conversational. Rooted in the moment and the person.
- **Copy angles:** 3-4 ways this product makes sense (each reflecting the actual energy).
- **Tone guide:** 2-3 examples of what sounds right + what sounds wrong.
- **Language guardrails:** What words to never use. What keeps it real.

### 4. PROTECT IT

Make simple rules:
- "We never use words like: ___"
- "We always sound like: ___"
- "We stay grounded by: ___"

## BRAND APPLICATIONS

### Landing Page Structure

1. **Hero Section:** What the product does, then how it feels. Lead with the moment.
2. **Why It Matters:** 2-3 angles of why this approach works. Show understanding, not features.
3. **How We Work:** Your process (the discovery conversation framework). Builds trust.
4. **Examples:** 2-3 case studies showing product → positioning translation.
5. **CTA:** Simple and direct. "Let's talk about your product" not "get started today."

### Email Voice

- **Onboarding:** Short, personal. Asks one good question.
- **Follow-ups:** Shows examples from other projects. Teaches something about positioning.
- **Nurture:** One insight per email about how to think differently about your brand.

### Social Voice

- Share observations about good products ("Notice how [product] doesn't feel like a corporation? That's intentional.")
- Ask questions that help developers think about their own work differently
- Celebrate examples of products with clear personality
- Never boastful. Never salesy. Just noticing what works.

### Work Samples / Case Studies

Instead of metrics, show transformation:

**The Product:** What they built (3 sentences)

**What We Heard:** What they told us about their product (in their words)

**The Translation:** What changed in positioning and copy

**What Happened:** Real feedback from people using it. What did they notice differently?

## WHEN TO BREAK THE RULES

Dlytful isn't rigid. The goal is consistency in feeling, not consistency in execution.

You can break guidelines if they serve the work better. You *can't* break them without losing what makes it real:

- Using corporate language
- Getting too clever or trying too hard
- Cold, aggressive colors
- Talking down to the people you're helping

## PROTECTING DLYTFUL

As you grow:

- **This isn't a template factory.** You work *with* people, understanding their specific situation. Each positioning is rooted in actual conversation.
- **Stay small on purpose.** More clients mean less understanding. A discovery call should feel like a real conversation, not a qualification call.
- **Only work with people you believe in.** If their product doesn't have integrity, the brand work won't either.
- **Show up as yourself.** People are hiring you. Not a faceless company. Not a system.
