# THE DLYTFUL DISCOVERY CONVERSATION
## Your Client Session Template (30-45 minutes)

---

## PRE-CALL PREPARATION

**Send this 24 hours before:**

"We're going to talk about what you've built—not the features, but what it actually does for people. Come ready to talk about the moment someone uses your product and thinks 'this is good.' That's where we start."

---

## PART 1: THE PRODUCT IN PRACTICE (10 minutes)

### Open with:
"Walk me through the moment someone uses your product and feels something click. Not the feature—the moment. What's happening for them?"

**Listen for:** The emotional experience. When they feel relief, clarity, ease, or delight. The actual context of a person's day.

### Follow-up questions:
- "What doesn't your product do that you're actually proud of? What did you leave out intentionally?"
- "If you explained this to a close friend—not a potential customer—in one sentence, what would you say?"
- "What would frustrate you most if someone misunderstood what you built?"

**Write down:** Their exact words. Metaphors. Emotional language. Specific moments.

---

## PART 2: THE PERSON WHO GETS IT (8 minutes)

### Ask:
"Who is the person that immediately understands what you've built? Not age and job title—who are they actually?"

**Listen for:**
- How they describe real people (not demographics)
- What pressures or frustrations drive these people
- What they value that isn't typical

### Follow-up:
- "What would they say about your product to their friends? Not the pitch—what would they actually say?"
- "What were they frustrated with before they found you?"
- "Are they trying to prove something to themselves or build an identity around something? What is it?"

**Write down:** Their language. Their context. What actually matters to them.

---

## PART 3: WHAT YOU'RE NOT (8 minutes)

### The important question:
"What do you absolutely not want your brand to feel like? What's the opposite of what you're doing?"

**Listen for:** The things they're rejecting. Corporate language. Tone they'd never use. What feels false to them.

### Examples to prompt:
- "Would you ever use words like 'solution' or 'leverage' in a sentence about your product?"
- "Does your brand feel like it was written by a person, or by a marketing department?"
- "Are you inviting people in, or trying to convince them?"

**Write down:** The NO LIST. What they're deliberately not being. These are your guardrails.

---

## PART 4: THE MOMENT (5 minutes)

### Ask:
"If you had to describe the feeling of a perfect day—the kind of day you mention to friends weeks later—what moment would it be? Morning? Golden hour? Something else?"

**Listen for:** Sensory details. Light. Pace. How it feels physically. The emotional temperature.

### Why this matters:
This isn't about summer days directly. It's about understanding the energy they want their product to create. The spaciousness. The effortlessness. The clarity.

**Write down:** The moment. The light. The pace. How it *feels*.

---

## PART 5: REAL WORLD TEST (5 minutes)

### Summarize back:
"Here's what I'm hearing. [Reflect their product's energy in 2-3 sentences using their words.]"

**Watch their response carefully.**
- If they nod and build on it = you've got it
- If they correct you = dig deeper into what you missed
- If they get animated = you've found something true

### Close with:
"When we translate this into how you talk about your product, will it sound like something the people who already love your work would recognize?"

**Write down:** Their confidence. What excites them most.

---

## AFTER THE CALL: SEND BACK THE TRANSLATION

**Within 24 hours, send:**

**[THEIR PRODUCT NAME] — WHAT WE HEARD**

**The Core Moment:** [The moment they described—how it feels]

**The Real Person:** [Who they're building for—in human terms]

**What They're Deliberately Not:** [The anti-pattern list]

**Draft Positioning (Working):**
For [their person], [product name] is [what it does] that [what makes it different] because [why it matters to them personally].

---

## DELIVERABLES YOU'LL CREATE

Based on the conversation, build:

### 1. POSITIONING STATEMENT
One sentence. Conversational. True to what you heard.

**Example:**
"For developers who want their tools to disappear into the work, [Product] is a deployment platform that just works because you didn't leave a corporate job to fight deployment."

### 2. THREE COPY ANGLES
Each angle is one way their product makes sense—rooted in what they actually do, not feature descriptions.

**Example angles:**
- "It works the way you expect it to, not the way they designed it"
- "It's built by someone who actually uses it"
- "It looks like someone understood what matters"

### 3. TONE GUIDE
Examples of what sounds RIGHT and what sounds WRONG.

**RIGHT:** "You built something good. The words should match."

**WRONG:** "Unlock your product's true potential with our strategic positioning solution."

### 4. VOICE RULES
Quick, practical rules.

- "We never use: solution, leverage, synergy, thought leader, seamless"
- "We always sound like: someone explaining something to a friend"
- "We stay real by: talking about actual moments, actual people, actual problems"

### 5. TEAM ONBOARDING
Share the conversation summary so everyone who writes for them speaks the same language.

---

## RED FLAGS

If they're answering with:

- **Jargon**: They're not clear yet. Ask "What does that actually feel like?"
- **Only features**: Redirect to "Why does that matter to you personally?"
- **Demographics only**: They haven't thought about real people. Help by asking "Who do you genuinely respect in your space?"
- **"Everyone is our customer"**: Push back kindly. "Everyone equals no one. Who's the first person this was for?"

---

## WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

By the end, you should have:

✓ One clear statement about what their product actually does

✓ A specific person you could write to (not a demographic, a person)

✓ A moment that captures what it should feel like to use it

✓ A clear list of what they're not

✓ Confidence that you understand the product better than they expected

---

## THE FEELING THEY SHOULD HAVE

After the call, they should think:

"This person understood what I was building better than I explained it. I trust them to put it into words."

If they don't feel that? You need another conversation. This understanding is everything.

---

## FOLLOW-UP: WEEKLY CHECK-IN

For clients you're working with longer-term:

"Did we stay true to what we heard this week? Anything that felt off? What actually landed?"

This keeps the translation honest and grounded in reality.

---

## THE SIMPLE TRUTH

Your job isn't to tell them what their brand should be. Your job is to help them say what it already is.

Listen. Understand. Translate. That's it.
