## What is a Brand Prompt?

A **Brand Prompt** is the deliverable. It's structured text that users paste into Bolt, Lovable, or Replit. It transforms their app's visual identity and voice without touching functionality.

Brand Prompts include:
- Positioning statement (who it's for, what makes it different)
- Visual direction (colors with psychological rationale, typography)
- Voice rules (sounds like, never sounds like, banned words)
- Brand energy (derived from their inputs)
- Archetype alignment (personality framework)

One paste. The app transforms.

## The Rules System: Deterministic + Probabilistic

### Why This Matters

Branding has hard rules that work. Positioning statements need a specific structure. Voice rules need specific categories. Color choices need psychological grounding.

But within those rules, every brand should feel unique. The language, metaphors, and specific choices should feel like *theirs*, not a template.

### Hard Rules (Deterministic)

These are non-negotiable. We enforce them silently.

**1. Positioning Template**
```
For [target audience], [brand name] is the [category] that [point of differentiation] because [reason to believe].
```
Structure never changes. AI fills the blanks. Users see it building.

**2. Voice Rules Categories**
Always three: `sounds_like`, `never_like`, `never_words`  
If AI doesn't populate all three, we add sensible defaults.

**3. 12 Archetypes Only**
Innocent, Sage, Explorer, Rebel, Magician, Hero, Everyman, Lover, Jester, Caregiver, Ruler, Creator.  
No AI-invented archetypes. These are proven frameworks.

**4. Color Psychology**
Every color suggestion includes *why*. Not just "blue"â€”"Clear blue (#007FFF)â€”evokes trust and reliability, grounding for a professional brand."

**5. Banned Words**
These never appear in outputs:
> synergy, leverage, solution, cutting-edge, innovative, seamless, robust, best-in-class, thought leader, disrupt, game-changing, paradigm, holistic, scalable, turnkey...

Silently removed. Users never see corporate jargon in their Brand Prompts.

### Soft Rules (Probabilistic)

These are AI-generated, user-controlled.

- Specific language within templates
- Metaphors and analogies from their inputs
- Copy angles (creative interpretations)
- The translation of "perfect day" â†’ brand energy
- Archetype recommendations (suggestions, not mandates)
- Social bio and one-liner variations

## Validation: Flags and Warnings

Users should always feel in control. We surface issues, explain rationale, let them decide.

### Quality Flags (Soft)

Inline hints that suggest improvements. Users can dismiss.

Examples:
- "Your positioning could be more specific. What makes you truly different?"
- "Your target audience might be too broad. Who specifically gets the most value?"
- "Voice traits like 'professional' don't differentiate. What specific personality should come through?"

**UI treatment:** Yellow/amber hint below the relevant section. "Dismiss" option.

### Contradiction Warnings

When choices create tension, we explain but allow.

Examples:
- "You selected 'Rebel' archetype but your positioning emphasizes 'reliability.' These can work together, but you'll want to be intentional about how rebellion shows up in a trustworthy way."
- "Your 'Sage' archetype typically sounds 'authoritative, knowledgeable, analytical.' Your voice rules don't obviously reflect this. This can work if intentional."

**UI treatment:** Warning card with "Keep my choices" and "Help me reconcile" buttons.

### Input Flags (Informational)

When user inputs contain jargon, we note it softly.

Example:
- User types: "We provide innovative solutions..."
- Inline hint: "Words like 'innovative' can feel corporate. We'll translate this into more human language."

**Not blocking. Just informing.**
